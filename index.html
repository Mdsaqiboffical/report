ALLAMA IQBAL

Allama Iqbal, whose full name is Muhammad Iqbal, was a renowned philosopher, poet, and politician in British India. He was born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, which is now in Pakistan, and he passed away on April 21, 1938. Iqbal is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in Urdu literature and is often referred to as Mufakkir-e-Pakistan (The Thinker of Pakistan) and Shair-e-Mashriq (The Poet of the East). Iqbal's poetry, written primarily in Persian and Urdu, explores themes of spiritual awakening, self-discovery, and the revitalization of the Islamic world. He was a strong advocate for the political and cultural rejuvenation of the Muslim world, emphasizing the importance of unity among Muslims.
